The Problem

It's not about effort. It's about a gap — on both sides.

"A skills gap and unequal access to opportunity are keeping people from economic mobility and employers from the talent they need — sustaining poverty and weakening the communities where the gap is widest."

The Human Side

  • People who want to build stable lives can't reach the careers in front of them
  • Opportunity is unevenly distributed across communities
  • Poverty persists and economic mobility stalls where the gap is widest

The Economic Side

  • Employers can't find the skilled talent they need to grow
  • Positions go unfilled, industries can't scale
  • Communities weaken economically on both sides of the equation

Goodwill's network of 150 organizations sits at the center of that gap — reaching millions every year.

The Opportunity

The network knows WHERE they stand. They need to know HOW to get there.

What Exists ✓

  • Updated adoption criteria
  • Logic model
  • Fidelity monitoring tools
  • Network-wide survey (114/148; 77%)
  • EPIS-based adoption framework

What's Missing

  • A progressive roadmap showing how to move from where they are to full adoption
  • Step by step, across every dimension of OA
  • With clarity on who is responsible for what

The OA Theory of Action fills that gap.

Two Frameworks, One Roadmap

EPIS shows where you are in the journey. AIF shows what you’ve built.

🗺️

EPIS — The Journey

Aarons et al., 2011

Exploration → Preparation → Implementation → Sustainment
Asks: Where are we in the adoption process?

🔧

AIF — What You Build

Fixsen / NIRN

Exploration → Installation → Initial Implementation → Full Implementation
Asks: Have we built what’s needed to succeed?

The EPIS stages map directly to OA adoption tiers: Explore and Prepare are pre-adoption — building toward readiness. Implement = OA Essentials (Gold) — delivering core services consistently. Sustain = OA High Fidelity (Platinum) — the full 21-area model with evidence-based tools and continuous improvement.

Together: a complete picture of how organizations move from fragmented programs to a coordinated, tiered system.

The Double Nested Logic Model

Two levels. One system. Outcomes at the network level become inputs at the local level.

This is a nested logic model — a standard methodology for national organizations that work through local sites. GII builds the framework, tools, and support at the network level. Those become the foundation that local Goodwill organizations use to deliver services and change lives. The University of Wisconsin Extension, StriveTogether, and the National Cancer Institute all use this same structure.
