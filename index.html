The Challenge

There's a gap in the workforce — and it hurts both sides.

👤 For People

  • People who want stable careers can't reach the opportunities in front of them
  • Access to training, support, and good jobs is unevenly distributed
  • Poverty persists where the gap is widest

🏢 For Employers

  • Employers can't find the skilled talent they need to grow
  • Positions go unfilled, industries can't scale
  • Communities weaken on both sides of the equation

Goodwill's network of 150 organizations sits at the center of that gap — reaching millions every year.
But across the network, organizations know where they stand. They need to know how to get there.

What We Know

We surveyed 114 of 148 Goodwill organizations (77% response rate). Three findings stand out.

48
Closer Than They Realize
48 organizations scored higher on implementation than their own self-assessment suggested. With the right reframing and support, they can move forward quickly.
#1
Barrier: Role Clarity
50–75% of members at every level — even the most advanced — say unclear role design is their biggest challenge. This is structural, not motivational.
Core vs.
Flex
What Members Want
Organizations want standardized tools and expectations, with flexibility to adapt to their local communities, populations, and funders.

March 2026 · n=114 · 77% response rate · Self-reported · OA Adoption Survey

The Missing Piece

We've built the foundation. Now we need the roadmap.

✅ What Exists

  • Updated adoption criteria (Gold and Platinum levels)
  • Logic model connecting activities to outcomes
  • Fidelity monitoring tools to measure quality
  • Network-wide adoption survey (114/148; 77%)
  • Research-based adoption framework (EPIS)

❌ What's Missing

  • A progressive roadmap showing how to move from where you are to full adoption
  • Step by step, across every dimension of OA
  • With clarity on who is responsible for what
  • With GII support embedded at every stage

The OA Theory of Action fills that gap.

How GII Supports Adoption

The Theory of Action shows what adoption looks like. These services are how organizations actually get there.

Three levels of support — matched to where each organization is in the journey. As organizations progress, the intensity of GII support increases.

🛠️

Self-Directed OA Adoption

Explore → Prepare
  • Utilize OA Landing Page on Goodwill Exchange
  • Complete OA Readiness Assessment
  • Review OA Logic Model and complete OA Fidelity Index
  • Draft OA Action Plan independently
  • Invited to OA Learning Events and OA Fellows program
Ongoing · At the organization's own pace
👥

Group OA Advisory Consultation

Prepare → Implement
  • Structured 2-month advisory consultation focused on closing key gaps
  • Develop an OA Action Plan with clear next steps
  • Follow-up cadence to ensure accountability for adoption
  • Peer learning with organizations at similar stages
2 months · Structured group format
🎯

Individualized OA Consultation

Implement → Sustain
  • Customized 4–6 month mission consultation for org-specific gaps
  • Utilize Fidelity Monitoring tool to validate OA Gold or Platinum
  • Develop OA Action Plan with follow-up cadence
  • Direct GII support calibrated to organizational context
4–6 months · Tailored to individual organization

The System Behind the System

This is a nested logic model — the same approach used by the National Cancer Institute and StriveTogether.
GII builds the framework. Local organizations deliver the services. People's lives change.

Level 1 — GII Builds the System

Inputs

OA model (21 areas), survey data, MDRC partnership, CN certification, OA Fellows, TA capacity, fidelity tools, MOLÉ

Activities

Develop Theory of Action, deliver 3-tier support, certify navigators, convene peer learning, partner with MDRC

Outputs

# orgs self-assessed, # in each support tier, # navigators certified, # Fellows active, sessions delivered

Network Outcomes

Orgs know where they stand; staff know their roles; common language; adoption progresses; impact story grows

⬇

Level 1 Outcomes Become Level 2 Inputs

Level 2 — Local Orgs Deliver Services

Inputs

Mission Leader, aligned staffing (8 roles), certified navigators, employer partners, data systems, GII support

Activities

Outreach, intake, assessments, career & financial plans, skills training, wraparound supports, job placement, retention, alumni

Outputs

# reached, # assessed, # with plans, # in training, # placed in jobs, # with follow-up at each interval, # alumni

Participant Outcomes

Matched services, clear plans, barriers addressed, skills built, quality employment, career advancement, financial stability
